Stories

Do you know that homelessness affects both people and the environment? These real-life stories show how displacement, poverty, and disasters create challenges for communities and sustainable cities.

Displacement in Kayah State

A mother and her two children from Kayah State were forced to leave their home after fighting broke out near their village. They first hid in the forest, but it was unsafe and difficult to survive. Later, they moved to a temporary shelter where many other displaced families are staying.

Life is crowded, and the children cannot attend school regularly. The family hopes to return home one day, but the situation remains uncertain.

This displacement also affects the environment because people living in forests may cut trees for shelter and firewood. This can lead to deforestation, waste buildup, and damage to wildlife habitats.

Migration Near Yangon Region

A father from a rural village moved with his family to the outskirts of Yangon after losing farmland due to conflict and difficult living conditions.

They now live in temporary housing and struggle with unstable jobs, high living costs, and limited access to clean water and food.

Rapid population growth in one area increases waste, pollution, and pressure on water and land resources. Informal settlements can also affect drainage systems and sanitation.

Flood Displacement in Delta Region

A family in Myanmar's delta region was forced to leave their home after severe flooding destroyed their house and farmland.

They now live in a temporary shelter with many other displaced families. Even after the water receded, their land remained damaged, making it difficult to restart farming.

Flood displacement harms the environment by damaging soil quality, spreading waste and pollution, and disrupting natural ecosystems.

Why These Stories Matter

These stories show how homelessness affects both people and the environment. When people do not have safe housing, they may live in temporary shelters, streets, parks, or abandoned places where proper waste systems are unavailable.

This can lead to pollution, waste buildup, and damage to natural environments. Understanding these challenges helps communities create cleaner, safer, and more sustainable cities for everyone.
